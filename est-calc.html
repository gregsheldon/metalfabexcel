1. True Hourly Labor Cost:

Most owners only look at the base salary, but the business pays much more. We use a "Burden Rate" to account for taxes, benefits, and insurance, then divide by the standard work year.

Formula: Salary × (1 + Burden Rate) ÷ 2,080 hours
2. Yearly Quoting Resources:

This calculates the literal cash spent on the act of manual data entry. If an estimator is re-typing data instead of solving technical problems, this is the cost.

Formula: (Bids per Week × 52) × Minutes per Bid ÷ 60 = Annual Hours
3. Error Risk (Cost of Inaction):

Manual entry is a magnet for typos. A 1% error rate is a conservative industry standard for manual spreadsheets. We calculate this by identifying the total Pipeline Volume your shop processes.

↳ Total Pipeline Volume: Bids per Year × Avg Job Size Formula: Total Volume × 0.01 Risk

When you reclaim time, you increase your Quoting Velocity. This math shows what happens when your estimator can bid more jobs without working more hours.

Logic: Reclaimed Hours × New Velocity × Job Size
